📚 Engineering Knowledge Base
💧 Moisture Content (MC)
Percentage of water in soil mass. Essential for lubrication of soil particles during compaction. Typical range: 5% - 25%.
⚖️ Dry Density (DD)
Mass of soil solids per unit volume. Higher dry density indicates better load-bearing capacity. Typical range: 1.4 - 2.1 g/cm³.
🎯 Optimum Moisture Content (OMC)
The specific moisture level where a soil can be compacted to its maximum possible dry density.
💡 Engineering Insights
🛠️ Compaction Tips
  • Compact in thin layers (lifts) for maximum efficiency.
  • Maintain moisture within ±2% of OMC for best results.
  • Use heavy vibratory rollers for granular soils.
  • Use sheep-foot rollers for cohesive clayey soils.
